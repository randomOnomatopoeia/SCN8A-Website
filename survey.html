4. Intellectual and Developmental Disability:
Mild IDD is characterized by IQ between 50 and 70 and having achieved almost all developmental milestones within a few months of the appropriate age.
Moderate IDD is characterized by IQ between 35 and 50 and have achieved between 50% and 75% of developmental milestones at the appropriate age.
Severe IDD is characterized by IQ less than 34, noticeable motor impairment and extremely limited development (i.e. achieving less than half of their developmental milestones at the appropriate age.)
Patients are neurotypical if they have experienced age appropriate development across all milestones and have no intellectual impairment.

7. Seizure Type:
Two phase seizure that starts with a rapid stiffening of muscles, followed by rpid, rhythmic jerking. Patient may cry out, lose consciousness, fall to the ground or have muscle jerks or spasms. These seizures typically last 1 to 3 minutes and often result in the patient losing consciousness. Also referred to as grand mal seizures.
Focal seizures are located in one area of the brain, causing twitching, temporary confusion or daze, or changes in taste or smell. Focal seizures include simple, complex, secondarily generalized, or gelastic (laughing) and dacrystic (crying) seizures.
Muscles in the patients body become stiff or tense. These seizures usually last less than 20 seconds and may result in falling if the patient is standing.
Short jerking spasms in parts of the body. These jerking motions may be localized to the neck, shoulders, and upper arms.
Parts or all of the body become limp. If standing, the patient falls to the ground. These seizures may also be called drop seizures.
Regular and sustained jerking movements lasting a few seconds to a minute.
Sustained rapid jerking or shaking of parts of one side of the body.
Brief flexion or extension of body parts that occur in clusters every 5-10 seconds over a 5-10 minute period.
Rapid blinking or staring spells that suddenly occur in the patient. Also referred to as petit mal seizures.
Staring spell lasting longer than 20 seconds. This seizure type is not typically preceded by rapid breathing. Other presentations include rapid blinking, chewing movements, lip smacking or jerking movements of lips.
Seizures may not include any physical effect on muscles, but are changes in mood, sensations or thinking. Patients may retain awareness and ability to interact or may lose awareness during these seizures.
Seizures induced by fever or increased body temperature due to exercise or exposure to heat. These seizures can either be full body convulsions or single body parts moving independently of others.
